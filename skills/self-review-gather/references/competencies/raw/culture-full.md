# Software Roles - Razorpay Values guidelines

## Tenets
Here are the tenets for this topic
- Razorpay values are the foundation of our culture and form the fundamental expectation of every employee at any level.
- Razorpay values are not specific to a business unit, a region, or location.
- Razorpay values are crafted to be timeless, but are evolved periodically to make sure they are relevant to changing requirements of industry and job expectations.
- Razorpay values are used by:
  - interviewers to make hiring decisions
  - all employees in the role for self evaluation and to identify areas of growth
  - peers to provide actionable feedback
  - managers in performance reviews and to provide career guidance to their employees

## Razorpay Values

### We are customer first
This company value emphasizes putting the customer at the center of everything.

### We take ownership
This value emphasizes individual initiative and responsibility.

### We challenge the status quo
This company value encourages constant innovation by embracing challenges, seeking unconventional solutions.

### We internalize agility and integrity
This company value emphasizes a strong bias for action, embracing change, and delivering on commitments.

### We are transparent
This company prioritizes radical transparency through open communication in public channels, encouraging everyone to actively participate and share information, blurring lines between confidentiality and inclusivity.

### Razorpay grows with Razors
This company value emphasizes individual and collective growth.

## Software roles level matrix

### We are customer first

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Ask "why" behind features: When assigned tasks, understand how they benefit the customer, not just technical requirements.
- Identify user pain points: During testing, actively look for areas where the software causes difficulty or confusion for users.
- Provide clear user documentation: Write concise and user-friendly instructions for features you work on.
- Propose user-centric solutions: When solving problems, suggest solutions that directly address user needs and pain points.
- Participate in user testing: Volunteer to observe and gather feedback during user testing sessions.
- Analyze user feedback: Read and understand user feedback reports, suggesting improvements based on their comments.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Advocate for user experience (UX): Promote usability and accessibility considerations during design discussions.
- Develop features with prototypes: Build low-fidelity prototypes to get early user feedback and iterate based on their input.
- Write user stories: Contribute to user stories that clearly define user needs and desired outcomes.
- Connect with Customers: Interact with at least 5 customers in a review cycle either via FreshDesk or shadowing support calls
- Conduct user interviews: Participate in user interviews to gain firsthand understanding of user needs and challenges.
- Contribute to customer support: Offer occasional assistance to the customer support team to gain insights into real-world issues.
- Champion A/B testing: Encourage A/B testing of different design options to determine what works best for users.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Connect with Customers: Interact with at least 5 customers in a review cycle either via FreshDesk or shadowing support calls
- Mentor on user-centric development: Guide junior engineers on incorporating user needs into their development process.
- Lead customer-facing projects: Take ownership of projects that directly impact the customer experience.
- Analyze user data: Utilize user data to identify trends and opportunities for improvement.

**Level 4 (Staff Engineer/Staff Product Engineer/Engineering Manager)**
- Connect with Customers: Interact with at least 5 customers in a review cycle either via FreshDesk or shadowing support calls
- Drive customer-centric initiatives: Propose and lead initiatives that focus on gathering user feedback and improving customer satisfaction.
- Build relationships with customer success teams: Collaborate with customer success teams to understand customer pain points and translate them into actionable development projects.
- Define customer-centric metrics: Work with product and marketing teams to define and track key metrics related to customer satisfaction.

**Level 5 (Principal Engineer I)**
- Connect with Customers: Meet at least 5 customers in person in a review cycle
- Shape the product roadmap: Influence the product roadmap by ensuring customer needs are central to every decision.
- Advocate for customer empathy: Foster a culture of empathy for customer needs across all departments.
- Partner with industry experts: Collaborate with customer experience consultants or researchers to gain deeper insights into user behavior.

**Level 6 (Principal Engineer II)**
- Connect with Customers: Meet at least 5 customers in person in a review cycle
- Build customer-centric partnerships: Establish partnerships with external organizations that share your commitment to customer satisfaction.
- Become a customer champion: Be a vocal advocate for the customer perspective within the organization and at industry events.
- Shape the future of customer experience: Contribute to industry discussions and standards around customer-centric software development.

**Level 7 (Principal Engineer III/Director of Engineering)**
- Connect with Customers: Meet at least 5 customers in person in a review cycle
- Leave a legacy of customer focus: Ensure your contributions and mentorship create a sustainable culture of prioritizing customer needs within the organization.
- Invest in customer education: Develop resources and programs that educate customers about the value and benefits of your products.
- Inspire the next generation: Mentor and inspire young engineers to build software that makes a positive impact on the lives of users.

**Level 8 (Senior Principal Engineer/Senior Director of Engineering)**
- Connect with Customers: Adopt at least 2 customers in a review cycle and support them throughout

### We take ownership

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Ask clarifying questions: Don't just wait for detailed instructions, actively seek understanding and suggest potential approaches within your comfort zone.
- Own your learning: Proactively identify opportunities to learn new skills relevant to your tasks and seek resources to acquire them.
- Propose small optimizations: Suggest minor improvements to existing code or workflows, taking ownership of implementing them effectively.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Offer alternative solutions: When encountering challenges, present well-researched options alongside potential drawbacks, advocating for the most efficient approach.
- Take initiative on small bugs: Actively fix minor bugs and document your process, learning from the experience and taking full ownership of the resolution.
- Seek stretch assignments: Volunteer for tasks slightly beyond your current skillset, demonstrating your willingness to learn and grow in your ownership.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Lead small improvement projects: Propose and lead mini-projects focusing on specific goals, demonstrating your ability to plan, execute, and deliver value while owning the process.
- Mentor junior engineers: Share your experiences and guide less experienced colleagues, encouraging them to take ownership of their tasks and learning.
- Develop self-assessment skills: Regularly evaluate your work, identifying areas for improvement and proactively seeking feedback to enhance your ownership.

**Level 4 (Staff Engineer/Staff Product Engineer/Engineering Manager)**
- Design with ownership in mind: Advocate for modular and maintainable code structures during design discussions, fostering collective ownership and accountability.
- Facilitate collaborative ownership: When encountering roadblocks, guide teammates towards identifying solutions together, encouraging shared ownership of the outcome.
- Track and share progress: Regularly communicate your progress on assigned tasks, highlighting challenges and proposing solutions, demonstrating transparency and ownership of your work.

**Level 5 (Principal Engineer I)**
- Empower others to take ownership: Delegate tasks clearly, providing guidance while allowing teammates the autonomy to own their execution and decisions.
- Champion ownership culture: Promote and advocate for a culture where taking calculated risks and learning from mistakes are encouraged, empowering ownership across teams.
- Provide constructive feedback: Offer feedback that focuses on learning and improvement, fostering a safe space for colleagues to take ownership and iterate on their work.

**Level 6 (Principal Engineer II)**
- Develop ownership frameworks: Design and implement processes that encourage and support ownership throughout the development lifecycle.
- Measure and track ownership impact: Identify metrics that reflect effective ownership (e.g., decreased rework, increased innovation) and track their progress.
- Coach and mentor senior engineers: Share your expertise on promoting ownership with senior colleagues, enabling them to better empower their teams.

**Level 7 (Principal Engineer III/Director of Engineering)**
- Advocate for ownership across departments: Collaborate with other departments (marketing, sales) to foster a company-wide ownership culture, aligning goals and responsibilities.
- Partner with external mentors: Engage with external coaching or consulting services to gain fresh perspectives and best practices on fostering ownership.
- Become an industry leader on ownership: Share your insights and experiences at industry events or publications, shaping the discussion around effective ownership practices.

**Level 8 (Senior Principal Engineer/Senior Director of Engineering)**
- Shape the company's ownership strategy: Contribute to shaping the company's overall approach to ownership, aligning it with business goals and employee development.
- Build an ownership ecosystem: Partner with HR and leadership teams to design and implement systems that reward and recognize ownership behaviors.
- Mentorship future leaders: Advocate for and guide young engineers and aspiring leaders, instilling the value of ownership and empowering them to become future champions.

**Level 9 (Distinguished Engineer/Vice President of Engineering)**
- Leave a legacy of empowered teams: Ensure your contributions and mentorship create a lasting culture where ownership drives innovation and success within the organization.
- Invest in ownership education: Develop resources and programs that educate and motivate employees at all levels to embrace ownership and its benefits.
- Inspire the next generation: Share your story and lessons learned with young engineers and students, encouraging them to take ownership of their career journeys and make a positive impact.

### We challenge the status quo

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Question assumptions: When assigned a task, ask why things are done a certain way. Explore alternative approaches, even if they seem simple.
- Suggest small improvements: Look for opportunities to optimize existing code or processes, even in basic functionalities.
- Explore new tools and technologies: Keep up with trends, and proactively suggest incorporating new tools or languages for relevant tasks.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Identify inefficiencies: Proactively analyze workflows and suggest solutions to streamline processes or improve code structure.
- Research alternative solutions: When encountering problems, research alternative approaches beyond the usual fixes. Present the findings and discuss their feasibility.
- Contribute to innovation discussions: Participate actively in brainstorming sessions and offer unconventional ideas, even if they seem risky at first.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Design innovative features: Propose original functionalities that enhance user experience or address market needs in unique ways.
- Lead small-scale experiments: Suggest and design micro-experiments to test new concepts and validate alternative approaches.
- Mentor junior engineers: Encourage a culture of questioning and exploration by coaching lower-level engineers to challenge assumptions.

**Level 4 (Staff Engineer/Staff Product Engineer/Engineering Manager)**
- Spearhead process improvement initiatives: Advocate for and lead projects that implement innovative solutions to improve development workflows.
- Develop proof-of-concept prototypes: Build functional prototypes showcasing new ideas, even if they require extra effort.
- Collaborate with external experts: Seek partnerships with industry thought leaders or academic researchers to explore cutting-edge solutions.

### We internalize agility and integrity

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Take initiative on small tasks: Don't wait for detailed instructions - proactively ask clarifying questions and start working on smaller tasks within your understanding.
- Experiment with solutions: When assigned a bug fix, suggest and test a simple solution alongside the standard approach. Discuss the results with your mentor.
- Offer quick feedback: Share immediate thoughts and concerns during design discussions, focusing on action-oriented suggestions.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Propose alternative approaches: When encountering challenges, present at least two potential solutions, highlighting their pros and cons, and recommend the most efficient path forward.
- Own your learning: Actively seek opportunities to learn new technologies or tools relevant to current projects and suggest their application for improvement.
- Champion small changes: Advocate for streamlining repetitive tasks or improving communication channels within your team, proposing specific actionable steps.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Lead micro-iterations: Volunteer to lead small improvement projects with defined goals and timelines, demonstrating agility and delivering results quickly.
- Refactor confidently: Identify areas in your codebase for improvement and propose refactorings with measurable benefits and minimal disruption.
- Advocate for data-driven decisions: When discussions stall, suggest gathering quick data points (user surveys, A/B testing) to inform the next action.

**Level 4 (Staff Engineer/Staff Product Engineer/Engineering Manager)**
- Mentor agile practices: Share your experience with junior engineers, encouraging them to take initiative, ask questions, and experiment with solutions.
- Automate repetitive tasks: Identify workflows or tests that can be automated and propose implementing tools or scripts to optimize time and effort.
- Challenge assumptions openly: During meetings, respectfully question long-held practices and propose alternative approaches, focusing on potential for improvement.

### We are transparent

**General principles for all levels:**
- Share relevant information proactively: Share information that impacts your team or projects through established channels, avoiding information overload.
- Be open to questions and feedback: Encourage colleagues to ask questions and provide feedback, creating a safe space for open communication.
- Respect privacy and confidentiality: Recognize that not all information needs to be public and respect boundaries around sensitive topics.

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Ask clarifying questions: Feel empowered to ask questions in team meetings or channels to gain understanding.
- Share basic project updates: Regularly give concise updates on your assigned tasks in designated channels.
- Provide feedback constructively: When appropriate, offer feedback on ideas or processes in designated channels, focusing on improvement.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Lead by example: Actively share relevant information about your work and encourage others to do the same.
- Organize information sharing sessions: Facilitate discussions or presentations to share broader project updates or learnings with the team.
- Advocate for clear communication channels: Champion well-defined and accessible communication channels for different types of information.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Mentor and coach on transparency: Guide junior engineers on effective and respectful communication practices.
- Implement transparency initiatives: Propose and champion initiatives that improve information accessibility and transparency across the organization.
- Promote healthy boundaries: Advocate for a culture that balances transparency with respect for individual privacy and psychological safety.

### Razorpay grows with Razors

**Level 1 (SDE/PDE 1/PDET)**
- Focus on learning: Identify specific skills or technologies relevant to your role and actively seek opportunities to learn them (online courses, tutorials, mentors).
- Take initiative: Look for small tasks within your project where you can contribute beyond your assigned duties.
- Seek feedback: Ask your mentor or senior colleagues for feedback on your work and actively implement their suggestions for improvement.

**Level 2 (Senior Engineer/PDE 2/SDET)**
- Contribute to discussions: Actively participate in design meetings and suggest alternative solutions, even if they feel unconventional.
- Propose improvements: Identify areas in your codebase or workflow that could be optimized and suggest concrete improvements.
- Seek new challenges: Volunteer for tasks outside your comfort zone to expand your skillset and knowledge.

**Level 3 (Lead Engineer/Lead Prod Engineer)**
- Mentor junior engineers: Share your knowledge and experience with less experienced colleagues, helping them grow their skills and understanding.
- Lead small projects: Take ownership of small projects or features, demonstrating your ability to plan, execute, and deliver results.
- Attend industry events: Participate in conferences, workshops, or meetups to learn about new trends and technologies in your field.

**Level 4 (Staff Engineer/Staff Product Engineer/Engineering Manager)**
- Contribute to architecture discussions: Share your perspective on technical architecture decisions, considering scalability and future growth needs.
- Identify emerging technologies: Research and evaluate new technologies that could benefit your team or projects, presenting a compelling case for adoption.
- Network with industry professionals: Build connections with individuals in similar roles at other companies to share best practices and learn from diverse experiences.

## FAQs

**Why is it expected for employees in software roles to work on Razorpay values?**
Razorpay values are the foundation of our culture and form the fundamental expectation of every employee at any level.

**How will I be assessed on each value?**
Assessment on Razorpay values is done the same way assessments are done against competencies: self review, and manager review that takes into account peer reviews.

**What's the weightage of culture values in my assessment?**
Razorpay values enable employees to achieve their goals easier as it helps organizations to operate in unison.

**What if I go above and beyond and follow culture practices above my role?**
Culture champions and warriors help the organization achieve more. This usually reflects in higher potential to grow within the organization.
