# IC Product Design Competency Framework

## Introduction

### Guiding principles
- **Impact and outcome focused:** The competencies are refined to help us focus on the outcomes rather than simply focusing on the activities.
- **Growth via impact and outcome:** Impact is the primary lever for growth. Impact on product and business outcomes via design, rather than just design execution/delivery is rewarded.
- **Transparency:** Establish the highest amount of transparency possible between people managers and individuals on role expectations, reviews and promotions across levels at Razorpay.
- **Building a highly competent design team:** As a team, we are growing into a world-class design team, and the framework here should act as a guiding principle towards that direction.

### What this framework is
- **Common Baseline:** This framework sets a common baseline across the org on expectations from different roles in Product Design.
- **Reference Guide:** The aim is to help managers and individual contributors understand how different skills can help you achieve the desired impact.

### What this framework is not
- **Self-serve growth plan:** The framework doesn't aim to be a self-serve tool to plan your growth. You are expected to work with your manager.
- **Completely objective:** While we have used scores and definitions in places, the aim is to be a reference guide.
- **Tool for optimising for skills:** The framework provides structure to how skills can be used to have an impact.

## Output v/s Outcome/Impact
- **Output** = deliverables (final designs, wireframes, research findings)
- **Outcome** = the difference the output makes (better conversions, improved NPS, influenced roadmaps)

## The Roles

### Themes
- PD (L1)
- PD II (L2)
- Senior PD (L3)
- Lead PD (L4)
- Staff PD (L5)

### Role Summaries

**PD (L1):** A strong design executor. Responsible for a great design output on their projects.

**PD II (L2):** A strong design executor + an intermediate product solver. Takes part ownership of product solutioning along with complete accountability for great design outcomes on their projects.

**PD II (L2) - Typical Charter:** Product Experience of POD

**Senior PD (L3):** A strong design & product problem solver. Accountable for the experience of their charter and end-to-end product outcomes on their projects.

**Lead PD (L4):** A strong design & product problem solver + an intermediate design leader. Co-owns the product success of their charter with product counterparts. (managerial path) Owns output & growth of 1-2 designers.

**Staff PD (L5):** A strong design & product problem solver & a strong design leader. Accountable for the success of their product area while leading the output from their design team.

### Problem Complexity by Level

**PD (L1):**
- Clearly defined user problem
- Validated problem statement
- Clearly defined product solution
- Often a feature within a product or a small product
- Needs end-to-end UX/UI solutioning
- Little overlaps with other products
- When a functional-only UI-UX can work

**PD II (L2):**
- Validated problem statement
- The product solution has a direction but is not detailed
- Often a feature within a product or a small product
- Few overlaps with other products to handle
- May require deep product domain context
- Requires design solution beyond just functionality, at a behavioural and visceral level

**Senior PD (L3):**
- Problem identified, may not be validated
- Large feature or a small product
- Requires deep understanding of the product+business side
- Significant overlaps with other products to handle
- Requires deep design solutions at functional, behavioural and visceral

**Lead PD (L4):**
- Opportunity identified, not validated or refined
- New product journey
- Requires deep understanding of all facets of BU and beyond
- A major product area
- Significant product overlaps across multiple teams

**Staff PD (L5):**
- Idea/opportunity identified, no clear problem statement
- Requires buy-in from leadership
- Requires in-depth understanding of multiple BUs and org goals
- Significant overlaps across multiple product-lines/BUs

## Core Competencies

### Product Thinking

#### Problem identification & prioritization

**PD (L1):**
Shows curiosity to understand the problem space, OKRs and projects by:
- Gaining domain understanding of payments, banking, lending, etc., as needed
- Competitor awareness and analysis

**PD II (L2):**
Supports progress towards achieving the OKRs by:
- Assessing/questioning the prioritized problems and their potential impact
- Bringing in insights from previous researches, data and recordings

**Senior PD (L3):**
Actively influences and contributes to meeting the OKRs and assisting in the prioritization of the right problems by:
- Bringing newly uncovered user needs to the table
- Deriving actionable insights out of data

**Lead PD (L4):**
Shares ownership in OKR planning and achieving it with the PMs by identifying and prioritising impactful problems within their domain through:
- Bringing stated and unstated user needs to the table
- Deriving user insights as and when necessary

**Staff PD (L5):**
Defines and owns the overall product and business direction, along with the OKRs for their charter by:
- Establishing a north star vision for their charter

#### Product Solutioning

**PD (L1):**
Plays the role of a diligent checker and builds their product solutioning skills by:
- Questioning with first-principles thinking

**PD II (L2):**
Ensures the right solution direction has been taken by:
- Asking the right questions from a user lens
- Validating and guiding product decisions through user insights

**Senior PD (L3):**
Actively contributes and (when needed) course-corrects product solutions by:
- Influencing product decisions via user insights

**Lead PD (L4):**
Co-owns the product solution with PM and leads product solutioning for experience-led projects through:
- Defining partial/complete solutions from scratch in a PRD

**Staff PD (L5):**
Owns end to end solution to all complexity-level problems

### UX Design

#### UX creativity

**PD (L1):**
Delivers user-friendly solutions by:
- Following established UX best practices
- Understanding of the user from previous researches/market analysis

**PD II (L2):**
Consistently delivers user-friendly solutions that meet benchmarks by delivering designs derived out of:
- Data and recording analysis of current user behaviour
- User insights gathered with guidance
- Validation of designs through hallway or user testing

**Senior PD (L3):**
Shows instances of raising the bar for experience quality and delivers delightful experiences by:
- Uncovering relevant user insights
- Solving for user delight, and not just enabling functionalities

**Lead PD (L4):**
Consistently raises the bar for user experience quality within the team by:
- Uncovering and addressing both stated and unstated user needs
- Consistently driving user delight in designs

**Staff PD (L5):**
Pioneers new standards for easy-to-use and delightful solutions across the organization by:
- Establishing innovative processes to solve complex problems and set new benchmarks

#### Functional Solutions

**PD (L1):**
With guidance, solves the use cases and covers all edge cases by:
- Understanding the current system and experience
- Jotting out the proposed user journey through information architecture or a block diagram

**PD II (L2):**
Solves all possible use and edge cases within the current scope of the problem through:
- Detailed understanding and jotting down of the ideal user journey
- Pre-mortem exercise to ensure 95-100% coverage of cases

**Senior PD (L3):**
Solves all possible use and edge cases while considering future cases for the problem and prioritizes basis impact<>effort with guidance

**Lead PD (L4):**
Solves for all possible use and edge cases while considering future cases for the problem and prioritizes basis impact <> effort, demonstrating an ability to independently make informed decisions.

**Staff PD (L5):**
Solves problems regardless of complexity and ambiguity and independently considers all possible cases in their solution - present or future

### UI Design

#### Aesthetic quality

**PD (L1):**
Creates pixel-perfect UIs that consistently meet baseline quality expectations by:
- Following basic principles of UI design
- Solid knowledge of UI elements such as typography, layout, colour theory, etc.
- Incorporation of latest UI patterns

**PD II (L2):**
Demonstrates instances of creating UI that elevate the standard within their team by:
- Catering to the tech literacy of the user persona type (basic v/s power) in UI patterns
- Exploring beyond basic UI elements, for example, transitions, micro-interactions

**Senior PD (L3):**
Consistently creates UI that elevate the standard within their team, setting a higher bar for quality

**Lead PD (L4):**
Seizes opportunities to create UI that set new organisational benchmarks by:
- Pushing boundaries of UI and micro-interactions
- Pioneering innovative design approaches

**Staff PD (L5):**
Seizes opportunities to create UI that set new industry standards

#### Usage of & contribution to design system

**PD (L1):**
Delivers consistent designs that adhere to UI/brand guidelines by:
- Effectively utilising the existing design system and using the existing components

**PD II (L2):**
Demonstrates instances of contributing to the Design system through:
- (as needed) Creation of new components
- (as needed) Enhancements to existing components

**Senior PD (L3):**
Assists team-wide consistent output by:
- Actively working with the Blade team for the creation of new components, central or custom
- Enhancements to the existing components

**Lead PD (L4):**
Takes a leadership role in driving the utilization of the design system by:
- Actively working with the Blade team for the creation of new components, central or custom
- Enhancements to the existing components

**Staff PD (L5):**
Takes a leadership role in driving the utilization of the design system

## Behavioural Competencies

### Communication & Collaboration

**PD (L1):**
Supports in keeping the project on track (especially from design) by:
- Communicating about updates and blockers in relevant forums
- Updating and resolving issues on Slack, stand-ups, 1:1s, etc.
- Consuming, synthesizing and prioritising feedback

**PD II (L2):**
Drives buy-in for their ideas with stakeholders, and is able to run design discussions or alignment with wider forums by:
- Conveying design decisions through a well-crafted presentation
- Driving product/design decisions through evangelisation of user insights within the POD

**Senior PD (L3):**
Drives buy-in and independently resolves conflicts with stakeholders, to ensure the team is aligned on the final deliverables

**Lead PD (L4):**
Champions experience-led initiatives/changes in the products, building compelling cases to rally the team behind them by:
- Evangelising user insights in forums like Open House, Town Halls, etc.

**Staff PD (L5):**
Establishes systems to enable other designers/team-mates for brainstorming, presentations & other comm-collab activities to drive success

### Ownership & Delivery

#### Ownership

**PD (L1):**
Owns the charter (project in this case) end-to-end by:
- Active involvement throughout the stage in DOPE

**PD II (L2):**
Ensures that the experience for the user in their charter is top-notch, through:
- Maintenance of and active work on a design backlog, beyond their day-to-day project

**Senior PD (L3):**
Owns the success of the product, through:
- Consistent attempt to find user problems, especially experiential problems
- Propose solutions and get those implemented

**Lead PD (L4):**
Owns growth/success of the product and the designers in their charter through:
- Owning overall product experience
- Driving design quality in any project that goes live in their charter through any designer

**Staff PD (L5):**
Sets up the processes and frameworks to enable the team and the product area to achieve success

#### Planning and Delivery

**PD (L1):**
Ensures timely delivery and implementation of designs by:
- Following established industry norms
- DOPE adherence across the process

**PD II (L2):**
Achieves on-time and quality implementation of projects by:
- Collaboratively planning with DEP, and coordinating with developers

**Senior PD (L3):**
Own quality execution and support planning+delivery for projects as per product/biz constraints by:
- Working backwards from POD goals
- Actively influencing sprint plans
- Challenging development timelines for better efficiency

**Lead PD (L4):**
Follow up on the POD's quarter plans consequently plan for design bandwidth. Drives excellence & improvements in planning, delivery, and implementation quality for their charter.

**Staff PD (L5):**
Drives delivery process improvements within the DEP group, optimizing efficiency and productivity through innovative methods.

### Leadership

#### Mentorship

**PD (L1):**
Aims for an understanding of the wider problem space by:
- Participating actively in feedback sessions with questions or clarification

**PD II (L2):**
Contributes to the growth of individual designers and team culture by:
- Sharing constructive feedback to help designers in the team
- Buddying new designers in the team

**Senior PD (L3):**
Mentors (on core skills) and actively guides designer(s) in the team with:
- Active reviews in buddy/office hours
- Feedback and assistance in strategic and stakeholder conversations

**Lead PD (L4) - IC Progression:**
Owns the design output quality for designer(s) by actively mentoring them in improving their skills delivering high-bar design outputs.

**Lead PD (L4) - Managerial Progression:**
Takes up manager role for 1-2 designers and starts owning their growth and workload.

**Staff PD (L5):**
Pushes design quality throughout the team by:
- Improving processes
- Mentoring individual designers to drive high-quality outcomes

#### Team Building

**PD (L1):**
Consistently participates in team-wide activities and contributes individually through items like:
- Blog posts
- Assistance with work on Design social
- Hiring: Shortlisting and interviews

**PD II (L2):**
Contributes to team-wide activities frequently with guidance on items like:
- External talks
- Contribution to wider activities like Town hall, Offsite, All hands, etc.

**Senior PD (L3):**
Active contribution to the team-building process by:
- Taking ownership of planning and running org-wide activities like Town hall, etc.
- Leading hiring drives

**Lead PD (L4):**
Contributes majorly to a team-building activity while also:
- Defining processes and strategy for team-building and branding
- Contributing to hiring process improvements

## FAQs

**Q: Do I need to be at the defined level for all impact areas?**
A: You need to meet at least 3 out of 4 core impact areas at baseline expectations. The shortfall in one can be made up by being at a higher score in another core impact area or 2 points in behavioural impact areas.

**Q: Different designers can have different peak skills?**
A: Peak skills help you create a differentiator and help you grow quickly into a mentor.

**Q: What happens if I do not get the opportunity to make an impact in certain areas?**
A: If you haven't had an opportunity to display a skill in your current cycle your previous evaluation stands.
