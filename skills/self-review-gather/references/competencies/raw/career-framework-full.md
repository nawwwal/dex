# Career Framework 3.0

## Foundational Principles

- **Impact based:** Razorpay's Framework is Impact based - they talk about how engineers at each level can create impact and drive outcomes within a team. We value the impact backed by the right behaviour more than just the behaviour itself.
- **Directional:** They help engineers understand how they should think and act to achieve the intended impact. They don't lay out detailed steps, but focus more on high-level directions.
- **Context:** Every team operates in a different Context. What is relevant for one team may not be relevant for the other.
- **Behaviour is important too.** But it alone doesn't suffice.
- Competencies should present a clear Direction within the right Context and right Behaviour to achieve the desired Impact.

## Engineering Competency Sections

### Engineering Excellence
Engineering excellence is the pursuit of building excellence at the craft of engineering. This includes every aspect of core engineering outcomes in terms of how we solve problems. It involves being efficient and effective in delivering outstanding experiences towards the products and customers.

### Leadership
Leadership is about how we lead and influence people. As engineers, we leverage leadership traits within their scope of influence and create an impact at Razorpay.

## Career Framework for each Role

### Software Engineer (Level 1)

**Impact:**
A Software Engineer creates impact by successfully delivering well-defined projects with high-quality code. Their impact is within the scope of their team.

**Engineering Excellence:**
An engineer at this level is expected to understand a well-defined feature requirement and follow best engineering practices while writing code. They come up with a clear technical solution with minimal help and complete their tasks in a reasonable time frame. They are aware of their team's OKRs and understand the business metrics that the product is moving. They should focus on learning about a specific product sub-area and managing it independently with high quality and integrity. They should continuously focus on improving their skills and adopting best engineering practices.

They proactively keep their stakeholders informed and avoid churn on deliveries. They empathize with the customers and solve problems with a customer and Razorpay first mindset. They also understand how their implementation affects and interacts with the rest of the product.

**Leadership:**
Contribute to various initiatives that help improve Razorpay and assimilate Razorpay's engineering culture. They collaborate with stakeholders to collectively achieve common goals.

### Senior Software Engineer (Level 2)

**Impact:**
A Senior Software Engineer is a proven engineer who is capable of understanding business requirements thoroughly and completing them in a way that is considered to be of high quality. The impact at this level is focused on complete ownership of a product area and the ability to drive one or more projects with low - medium complexity independently end to end.

**Engineering Excellence:**
An engineer at this level demonstrates high ownership in continuously improving the products their team is responsible for. They have a good grasp of the product domain and actively participate in product conversations giving meaningful inputs to the specs and artifacts (code, PRD, design, schema etc). They are well aware of the OKRs surrounding their team and products. They question why certain OKRs are picked over others. It is fundamental that they understand the capabilities and limitations of the product and effectively communicate the same to stakeholders.

They are able to scope a well-defined problem and can independently come up with different solutions. They understand the tradeoffs of each solution and come up with a clear execution plan. They collaborate with stakeholders, build confidence and independently drive small projects and ensure its successes with operability in mind.

They understand what tech debt is and constantly think of ways to reduce any existing tech debt. They religiously follow stated best practices for the team and enable these practices for junior engineers. They learn from their peers, set a high benchmark for themselves and improve on these practices.

As a senior engineer in the team, they continuously strive to improve the stability of production systems. They own the operational aspects of engineering ensuring the high availability of their products and learning from RCAs. They empathize with the customers' problems and strive towards a quicker resolution to incidents and bugs. They are also able to drive the conversations during any incident and ensure their proper closures.

**Leadership:**
A senior software engineer is capable of delivering feedback to their peers and manager. They onboard and mentor new engineers comfortably and are the go-to person for what the product does and how the team operates. They actively work towards building a high performing team and improving the overall quality with agility. They undertake and contribute to various initiatives that help build and improve Razorpay culture.

As an engineer progresses through this role, it is important that they start thinking of the team's success and not just individual accomplishments.

### Lead Software Engineer (Level 3)

**Impact:**
A Lead Software Engineer creates impact by working towards their team goals and delivering them with a high-quality standard. They own a product area, undertake open-ended problems aligned towards business goals and come with well-defined strategies to execute them. They leverage their influence within their team to improve and maintain high engineering standards.

**Engineering Excellence:**
A Lead Engineer owns a product and has developed a deep thorough understanding of every aspect of it. They have visibility of the long term roadmap, OKRs and should have strong opinions on how the product should shape up.

They should be able to come up with an end-to-end solution for a high-level problem requirement. They further partner with the stakeholders to ensure the correctness and need of the solution. They effectively communicate problems, goals and solutions to the team and set a clear roadmap to execute. They are well aware of the product challenges and customer problems, and actively come up with efficient solutions to overcome them. They demonstrate and promote customer-first thinking within the team.

They have a good command over various building blocks of technology. They reuse org-wide existing patterns and keep themselves abreast with the technical aspects in other groups (platform, data, DevOps, other BUs). They can identify the complexities of domain and scale, and build systems after evaluating tradeoffs between different designs and product outcomes, based on the constraints of time, execution and other factors. They hold themselves and the team accountable for the successful delivery of the milestones. They take the product to a successful outcome.

They proactively identify and fix gaps in the system that has a business impact. They track and prioritize tech debts to continuously improve the product. They are the gatekeepers for incident management, operability, sustenance and quality. They should constantly be building best practices across their domain.

**Leadership:**
This is a critical juncture in their career path where they need to build and demonstrate the right leadership qualities. They work with multiple people in and across teams exhibiting high collaboration and ownership. They value the team's success over individual success.

As a lead, they represent the team in the wider org. They give constructive feedback with empathy to peers and their managers. They have a pulse of the overall health of the team. They frequently think of better ways to onboard new engineers into the team and ensure a great experience while doing so. They constantly seek feedback from peers and managers to look for opportunities to grow.

### Staff Engineer (Level 4)

**Impact:**
A Staff Engineer role has stepped into this role in an organic way by increasing breadth in Engineering Excellence. They create an impact in more than one aspect of Engineering. They influence key product and technology decisions across teams working closely with leaders and stakeholders. They continuously improve both the technical and operation posture of their product. They act as a Role Model in every aspect of engineering.

**Engineering Excellence:**
A Staff Engineer has a deep knowledge of the nuances of their customers and product. They own multiple areas of the product. They work with stakeholders to create, influence and own the roadmap spanning across multiple products. They go beyond the mentioned problem statement to own the overall success of the product. Being aware of the key product metrics, they proactively suggest ways to improve them.

They own OKRs and lead multiple initiatives to improve their product. They evangelize and help engineering and product leaders with the adoption of better technology and practices. They gain mastery in a specific technological area and leverage it to improve an existing product. They set objectives and roadmaps through OKRs and work with leads across teams to accomplish them. They are able to strategize between short term to long term solutions, based on the constraints of time, execution and other factors.

They should be able to take complex and open-ended problems in the product and the tech domain. They come up with a detailed roadmap or strategy to deliver the product. They simplify a complex problem to get more done with less. They can effectively communicate the nuances of problem-solving and tech depth needed to solve a problem.

A Staff Engineer owns and executes at least one major initiative in-depth. They give consistent, relevant and focused updates regarding their area of ownership to the stakeholders without any external help. They effectively facilitate cross-team work and their impact is felt across the org. They go beyond development and work with product, design, support, business, etc to onboard the product after taking it to live.

They keep track of key performance metrics of their product and lead initiatives to improve them. They are able to lead major incidents RCAs independently and drive their effective closure with different teams and stakeholders. They identify major tech debts within their product and continuously resolve them and help teams to plan and create a roadmap for it and prioritize it.

**Leadership:**
A Staff engineer proactively contributes to the betterment of other engineers in the company, through sponsoring, mentoring, and providing constructive, candid feedback. They are aware of the challenges within their team, seek suggestions and work towards improving them. They continuously look for ways to raise the bar for engineering.

They constantly strive to improve the productivity of engineers in the team. They actively mentor, give feedback and set good examples for the team to improve their skills and grow. They further onboard PMs and leaders onto the product.

They collaborate with other senior engineers across the company to push and accomplish larger company goals. They improve collaboration across teams by identifying and fixing gaps.

### Principal Engineer (Level 5)

**Impact:**
Principal Engineer sets and owns long term and short term product and tech roadmaps for their team. They create impact by leading initiatives that help build a world-class engineering culture at Razorpay. They leverage their experience and skills to improve the overall Razorpay culture. They act as a role model in every aspect of engineering.

**Engineering Excellence:**
A Principal Engineer owns multiple complex and open-ended problems that may spread over multiple quarters in the product and the tech domain. They identify and evangelize key product and technology goals for their team. They understand the vision for delivering large business goals that have multiple constraints. They are able to anticipate the challenges and influence the long term and short term roadmap for their teams and also influence the roadmap of other teams to execute this vision. They leverage their deep understanding of the product and insights about customers to evangelize customer-first thinking among other engineers and leaders.

A Principal Engineer is still acting in a very hands-on role building deeper technical expertise and leading at least one major initiative across a team. At this stage, they should also start focusing on increasing their breadth across tech and products. They own multi-quarter roadmaps and are deeply involved in setting up OKRs for large initiatives spanning multiple products. They execute their projects with very high standards, proactively identifying any risks and influencing tech directions to them. They are able to bring clarity on complex problems to stakeholders and guide the team towards a solution that is a magnitude better than the current. They constantly communicate with fellow senior engineers to assimilate, internalize and evangelize common best practices across their domain of influence. They are able to leverage new technical advances in improving every aspect of the product and engineering. They are able to do this by creating an environment that supports experimentation and iteration towards audacious goals.

They exercise judgment and take a stand that favors the priorities of the wider engineering org rather than favoring locally optimal outcomes. They are also the gatekeepers of quality within their domain. They continuously strive to improve the Quality posture of the team by leveraging existing tools or practices or promoting new ones. They demonstrate high empathy towards customers' problems and proactively work towards resolving them independently or with the team.

**Leadership:**
A Principal engineer proactively contributes to the betterment of other engineers in the company, through sponsoring, mentoring, and providing constructive, candid feedback. They are aware of the challenges within their team, seek suggestions and work towards improving them.

They are able to sell long term technical vision to the engineers for the product in their areas. They possess excellent verbal and written communication skills. They collaborate with other senior engineers across the company to push and accomplish larger company goals. They improve collaboration across teams by identifying and fixing gaps. They should also work with other leads and managers to set policies and processes that span engineering.

They constantly come up with initiatives that will help us build a great team that we would be proud to be a part of. They look for ways to continuously improve the productivity of engineers, through technology or more efficient operational processes.

They should lead or be a part of technical councils and help create Razorpay brand awareness around technological thought processes and open source contributions to attract good talent.

### Senior Principal Engineer (Level 6)

**Impact:**
A Senior Principal Engineer creates an impact by owning initiatives that are critical to the organization. They help with strategy and roadmap for a unit and effectively drive discussions to influence critical decisions at the org level. They bring new ideas to the table from within or outside that create a wide impact across the organization.

**Engineering Excellence:**
Senior Principal engineers demonstrate deep expertise in at least one specific technology and domain area in the organization. They have an understanding of domain knowledge of multiple products across the org and can think about how they can work together. They go beyond Razorpay's current scope, work with leading practitioners to identify new industry trends to the organization.

They understand the business at an org level and they proactively come up with detailed engineering solutions to large complex business problems. They are able to use their experience and knowledge to arrive at a solution faster through experimentation. They can take technical decisions which have a sweeping effect across the organization or multiple products.

They are responsible for an existing vertical and should be able to take up any new business vertical and deliver a high-quality roadmap, structure it well and push it to production without hassles. They own multi-year roadmaps and are deeply involved in setting up OKRs for large initiatives spanning multiple BUs and products.

They create architecture that shapes large parts of our business with a built to scale mindset. They have worked in-depth on multiple large pieces of technology and should be SMEs on multiple core technological domains.

**Leadership:**
Senior Principal engineers connect with peers in other organizations and bring their learning that helps grow Razorpay. They are able to create positive change in the organization, help others understand the need and continuously drive adoption. They are also the drivers of engineering culture change. They create the rails that ensure technology cadence across teams.

They identify, encourage and evangelize different tech initiatives at BU and Org level. They are constantly trying to question, identify and quantify problems that hamper developer experience across teams. They set high-level goals for the BU and work to drive it with other senior engineers and leaders in the team.

They also understand the importance of branding activities and inculcate these practices in team members. They themselves give talks and presentations to ensure good culture seeps into the system. Actively works towards identifying and representing Razorpay in national and international conferences. They are able to hire or attract good engineers and engineering leaders to the company.

Senior Principal Engineers have great Org design skills and collaborate with engineering leaders to define the structure of teams and units that are required to achieve business goals. They also actively participate in related activities, like AOP planning, to ensure these are achieved on time.

They are engaged in coaching Leads, Staff engineers and Principal Engineers. They are able to create a framework and work with the engineering managers to identify the scope and career growth of senior engineers in their teams. They work closely with engineering leaders in terms of creating value for engineers, identifying mentoring opportunities.
