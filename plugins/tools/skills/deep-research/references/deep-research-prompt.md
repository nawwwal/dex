# Deep Research Analysis Prompt

Analyze all provided sources (YouTube videos and articles) and create a comprehensive research summary following this 10-section framework. Your analysis should synthesize insights from ALL sources, citing specific videos and articles throughout.

## Analysis Framework

### 1. Key Themes & Core Concepts
Identify the 5-7 fundamental themes that emerge across all sources. For each theme:
- Define the core concept clearly
- Explain why it matters
- Show how multiple sources address it (cite at least 3 sources per theme)
- Highlight any evolution or progression in understanding

### 2. Best Practices & Methodologies
Extract actionable best practices that appear across sources. For each practice:
- State the practice clearly
- Explain the reasoning behind it
- Provide concrete examples from sources
- Note which experts/sources recommend it and their credibility
- Indicate consensus level (unanimous, majority, split opinion)

### 3. Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
Document frequently mentioned mistakes, anti-patterns, or pitfalls:
- What specifically to avoid
- Why it's problematic (consequences, costs, risks)
- Real-world examples or case studies from sources
- How to recognize you're making this mistake
- Recommended solutions or alternatives

### 4. Expert Insights & Notable Quotes
Capture high-value insights from authoritative sources:
- Direct quotes that crystallize key ideas
- Counter-intuitive findings or surprising revelations
- Insights unique to specific experts
- Memorable frameworks or mental models
- Include attribution (speaker/author, source title, timestamp if available)

### 5. Practical Applications & Use Cases
Ground the research in real-world application:
- Concrete scenarios where concepts apply
- Step-by-step implementation guidance
- Industry-specific adaptations
- Success stories and case studies
- Tools, frameworks, or templates mentioned

### 6. Emerging Trends & Future Directions
Identify forward-looking themes:
- New developments in the field
- Evolving best practices
- Predicted future changes
- Areas of active innovation
- Skills or knowledge that will become more important

### 7. Technical Details & Specifications
Capture specific technical information:
- Precise definitions and terminology
- Technical specifications or requirements
- Configuration details, code patterns, or formulas
- Version-specific information
- Platform or tool-specific guidance

### 8. Points of Disagreement
Acknowledge where sources diverge or debate:
- Identify contested topics
- Present multiple perspectives fairly
- Explain the reasoning behind each viewpoint
- Note which approach has more support
- Suggest criteria for choosing between approaches

### 9. Knowledge Gaps & Open Questions
Recognize limitations of current understanding:
- Questions raised but not answered
- Areas where sources provide contradictory information
- Topics mentioned but not explored deeply
- Acknowledged uncertainties or ongoing debates
- Suggestions for further research

### 10. Learning Progression
Create a recommended learning path:
- Foundational concepts to master first
- Natural progression from basic to advanced
- Prerequisites and dependencies
- Suggested sequence for exploring topics
- Resources categorized by difficulty level

## Output Format Requirements

**Structure:**
- Use markdown with clear heading hierarchy (H2 for sections, H3 for subsections)
- Include a table of contents after the introduction
- Use bullet points, numbered lists, and callouts for readability

**Citations:**
- Cite sources in context: [Video Title](URL) or [Article Title](URL)
- For videos, include approximate timestamps when referencing specific points
- Use blockquotes for direct quotes
- Create a "Sources Cited" section at the end

**Quality Standards:**
- Comprehensive: 3,000-5,000 words minimum
- Balanced: Represent diverse perspectives fairly
- Actionable: Readers should know what to do next
- Well-organized: Easy to scan and navigate
- Evidence-based: Claims supported by source citations
- Synthesized: Combine insights across sources, don't just summarize individually

**Tone:**
- Professional but accessible
- Objective and analytical
- Helpful and instructive
- Direct and clear

## Introduction

Begin with a 2-3 paragraph introduction that:
1. Defines the research topic and scope
2. Summarizes the source landscape (types of sources, perspectives represented)
3. Previews the key findings and major themes

## Conclusion

End with a 2-3 paragraph conclusion that:
1. Summarizes the most important takeaways
2. Provides actionable next steps for readers
3. Suggests how to go deeper on specific topics
