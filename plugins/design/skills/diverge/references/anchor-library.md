# Anchor Library

A curated cross-domain library of real, named references for the **Anchor** and **Delight moment** fields. Each entry is a specific, Googleable thing — a particular game mechanic, a particular installation, a particular ritual. Not a category, not a vibe.

The library is biased AWAY from software on purpose. The model already knows Apple Music, Stripe, Slack, GitHub. This list exists to remind it of the other 99% of human culture.

Use this in Step 3 (Generate concepts) alongside the axes and provocations. The bucket quota in `SKILL.md` requires the output set to draw from at least four of the ten buckets below.

Each entry: `Reference name — one sentence on the mechanic or moment it provides`.

---

## Video games

Stardew Valley friendship hearts — silent cumulative score visible above each villager; rises by living near them, not by completing tasks for them.
Animal Crossing K.K. Slider Saturday show — recurring scheduled live event at 8pm on Saturday; you have to be there or you miss it.
Hades Mirror of Night — permanent meta-progression where each death lets you rebuild yourself a little stronger; failure is the input.
Disco Elysium Thought Cabinet — you "internalize" ideas over real in-game time; commitments cost slots and pay off with passive perks.
Death Stranding chiral network — your ladders, ropes, and bridges appear in other players' worlds; you build infrastructure for strangers.
Outer Wilds knowledge-as-progression — the only thing that persists across the time loop is what YOU now know; the game ends when you understand.
The Sims wants and fears stack — needs surface as a small panel of changing icons that pull the character forward.
Civilization tech tree — branching dependencies where unlocking X requires Y and Z; visible long-term planning surface.
Fortnite battle pass — 100-step ladder bought at the start of a season; each step a small new cosmetic, climbable through play.
Hollow Knight bench-as-save-point — rest at a bench restores you and commits progress; every bench is a relief and a checkpoint.
Pokémon Center heal jingle — short signature audio + dialog box that signals "you are now safe"; same chord pattern across all games.
Tetris piece queue — three upcoming pieces always visible to the right; you plan, not just react.
Souls-game bonfire rest — kneeling at a bonfire commits world state, respawns enemies, refills flasks; the rest is also a reset.
Disco Elysium skill-check dice rolls — visible dice on screen with modifiers; you watch your own competence be tested in public.
Stardew Valley ginger island parrot perch — silent NPCs that exchange one currency for one piece of progress; nothing said, lots done.
Slay the Spire deck-shaping — each fight ends with a card-pick from three options; the deck is the character.

## Arts and installation

Olafur Eliasson Weather Project — an artificial sun in the Tate Turbine Hall; visitors lay on the floor and stared up at it for hours.
Yayoi Kusama Infinity Mirror Rooms — small room of mirrors and lights you stand in for 60 seconds; queue is the cost, awe is the payoff.
James Turrell Skyspace — a perfectly cut hole in a roof framing the sky at sunrise/sunset; the artwork is the sky itself, time-windowed.
Banksy Walled Off Hotel — the worst-view hotel in the world, opposite the West Bank wall; the experience IS the political statement.
Christo and Jeanne-Claude wrapping — temporary covering of buildings/landmarks; the wrap exists for two weeks then disappears forever.
Sol LeWitt wall drawings — instructions, not objects; whoever follows the instructions makes the artwork, certified by signed paper.
Marina Abramović The Artist Is Present — Abramović sat at a MoMA table for 736 hours; visitors sat across from her one at a time.
Lawrence Weiner statement-as-art — typeset sentences on a wall ARE the artwork; the work is the language.
teamLab Borderless rooms — projection-mapped digital ecosystems that respond to your presence; the artwork only exists when you walk in.
Andy Goldsworthy ephemeral sculptures — leaves, ice, stones arranged outdoors and photographed once before nature undoes them.
Rachel Whiteread House — a concrete cast of the inside of a demolished home; the absence is the form.

## Cinema and TV

Wes Anderson chapter cards — typeset interstitial that announces the next scene; symmetrical, deadpan, color-keyed.
Studio Ghibli food close-up — a single 4-second shot of cooking or eating; the meal feels real because the animation slowed down.
Twin Peaks Red Room curtains — a backstage liminal space with red velvet, chevron floor, backwards speech; the curtain marks the threshold.
Severance Lumon doors — identical white doors and corridors where space is intentionally illegible; the office is the prison.
Tarkovsky long takes — single shots that hold for 5+ minutes without cutting; the patience is the argument.
Better Call Saul cold opens — flash-forward in black and white before the title card; the future leaks before the present catches up.
Edgar Wright cuts on action — match-cut a kettle whistle to a dial tone; rhythm replaces dialogue.
Bergman silence beats — a face holds for 30 seconds with no music or movement; the silence does the heavy lifting.
Spike Lee double dolly — character and camera move together so the world appears to slide past while the person stands still.
The Office talking head asides — a glance straight into the camera mid-scene that says "you saw that too, right?" without words.
Studio Ghibli "ma" pause — the empty beat between actions, an animated breath before the next move.

## Literature and fiction

Borges Library of Babel — an infinite library containing every possible 410-page book; the search becomes the horror.
Calvino Invisible Cities — Marco Polo describes 55 imaginary cities to Kublai Khan; each city is a different lens on one real city.
Pratchett footnotes — gags hidden in footnote markers that pay off three pages later; the margin is part of the joke.
Murakami parallel-Tokyo wells — a literal hole in the ground that connects to a different version of the same place.
Le Guin Earthsea true-name magic — knowing a thing's true name lets you command it; secrecy of name = power.
Gaiman Sandman dreams library — a library containing every book ever dreamed but never written.
Hofstadter strange loops — self-referential structures (Bach fugues, Escher staircases) where the system describes itself.
House of Leaves typography — text that runs in spirals, corners, mirrors as the house's geometry breaks down.
Italo Calvino "If on a winter's night" — every other chapter is the start of a different novel; the reader is the protagonist.
Borges Garden of Forking Paths — every choice splits the timeline; all paths exist simultaneously.

## Music

Brian Eno Music for Airports — generative ambient designed for shared public space; never the same listen twice.
Steve Reich phasing — two identical loops drift slightly out of sync until they become a third pattern.
John Cage 4'33" — three movements of silence; the audience and room ARE the composition.
Aphex Twin face-hidden mystique — refusal to do interviews / show face becomes its own brand.
Club DJ peak-time build — 16-bar tension build with filter sweep + drum drop; the body knows what's coming.
Church antiphon call-and-response — leader sings a line, congregation responds; participation is the worship.
Indian raga time-of-day — specific ragas are only played at dawn / dusk / midnight; the song belongs to the hour.
Carnatic kriti structure — pallavi → anupallavi → charanam, with improv space inside each section.
Pink Floyd's vinyl-side breaks — albums designed so flipping the record is part of the experience.
Mixtape ordering — opener / closer / "track 5 vibe" / hidden bonus; sequence is meaning.

## History and ritual

Mughal jharokha — the king appeared at a balcony at a fixed daily time; if you didn't show up, you missed your chance to be seen.
Edo period sentō — public bath houses where social status flattened; everyone naked, no titles.
Library of Alexandria scroll borrowing — visiting ships had to surrender their scrolls for copying; the original stayed.
Roman milestone stones — every Roman mile marked by a stone with the distance to Rome; infrastructure as a constant nudge.
Silk Road caravanserai — fortified rest stops every 30km; a network of safe nodes made the trade possible.
Versailles morning levée — the king dressed in public; nobles competed for the privilege of handing him his shirt.
Quaker meeting silence — gather in a circle, speak only when moved; structure is the silence.
Buddhist takuhatsu — monks walk the streets at dawn, stand silently with bowls; locals come to them.
Shabbat candle lighting — Friday at sunset, every household lights two candles; the day pivots on the same act everywhere.
Day of the Dead ofrenda — household altar built for one night; food, photos, marigolds; absence becomes presence.
Tea ceremony grace — every gesture choreographed across 90 minutes; the host and guest are co-performers.
Japanese hanami — annual cherry blossom viewing; you go to one specific tree, with one specific group, one week a year.

## Sport and movement

F1 pit stop — eight-person choreography under three seconds; everyone has one job, the timing is the entire performance.
Biathlon — cross-country ski to a shooting range, hit five targets with a heart rate of 180; failure adds penalty laps.
Freediving descent — single breath, target depth, controlled spiral down a line; the line is the safety.
Curling sweepers — once the stone is thrown, two sweepers control its path with brooms; the throw is one fifth of the action.
Olympic relay handoff — the baton must change hands inside a 20m zone; every Olympics there's a dropped baton story.
Sumo opening salt throw — wrestler throws salt to purify the ring before each match; the ritual sells the seriousness.
Cricket bowler's appeal — bowler turns to umpire arms-spread shouting "howzat?"; the question is the appeal.
Cheerleader pyramid call — captain calls "ready", "okay"; everyone moves on the count, no individual hesitation.
Golf shot routine — same nine-step pre-shot every time, regardless of pressure; the routine IS the calm.
Boxing corner work — 60 seconds between rounds where one person is repaired by three; the corner is louder than the fight.

## Architecture and craft

Tadao Ando concrete walls — board-formed concrete with imprint of wood grain; cheap material treated like silk.
Nakagin Capsule Tower — modular pod hotel where each pod could theoretically be replaced; architecture as a kit.
Soviet bus stops — wildly diverse bespoke shelters in former USSR republics; every village had a unique one.
Zaha Hadid swooping ramps — handrails and floors that flow into each other; no right angles, no joints.
Kintsugi gold seams — broken pottery repaired with gold lacquer; the break becomes the most precious part.
Washi paper layering — translucent paper layers built up over wood frame; light passes through but vision doesn't.
Glassblower's pace — three minutes to shape molten glass before it hardens; rhythm is non-negotiable.
Barn-raising — a community gathers for one day to assemble a whole barn; one-day construction as social event.
MUJI store layout — almost no signs; products communicate function visually; quietness as the brand.
Japanese ryokan tatami switch — guests change from outdoor shoes to slippers to tatami in three transitions; thresholds carry weight.
Persian rug knot density — value is measured in knots per square inch; an invisible metric you can feel.

## Domestic and social

Family WhatsApp group — silent for days, then 40 messages in 10 minutes when one person posts a photo.
Calendar fridge magnets — physical calendar held by the fridge; whoever holds the marker controls the schedule.
Christmas lights timer — lights on at 5pm, off at 10pm; same neighborhood, same hours, no coordination needed.
Bedtime story routine — same five steps every night; the routine is the safety.
Airport arrival gate — sliding doors open and people you love walk out; no other moment in life works like this.
Barber shop running conversation — multi-month thread that picks up where it left off three weeks ago.
Corner store "regular" recognition — owner doesn't ask what you want; they already know.
College canteen group ordering — one person collects rupees, places one big order; coordination via trust.
Potluck label-the-dish — every dish has a sticky note with name + ingredients + dietary tags; informal but binding.
Holi color powder — once a year, complete strangers throw color on each other; the day suspends usual rules.
Indian wedding sangeet — choreographed family dances learned in two weeks of evening rehearsals; nervous joy.
House party kitchen huddle — the deep conversations happen near the snacks, never in the living room.

## Mythology and folklore

Trickster figures (Loki, Anansi, Hanuman, Coyote) — characters who progress by breaking rules; the rule-break is the gift.
Hero's journey beats — call → refusal → mentor → threshold → trials → return; the structure of every story.
Norse Yggdrasil — the world tree connecting nine realms; geography as narrative device.
Hindu pantheon roles — Brahma creates, Vishnu preserves, Shiva destroys; functions distributed across personalities.
Psychopomp guides (Hermes, Anubis, Yama) — figures whose only job is to escort souls across thresholds.
Oracular Pythia at Delphi — high priestess on a tripod over volcanic vapors, speaking in riddles; ambiguity is the answer.
Japanese yōkai signage — local spirits posted at borders, bridges, abandoned buildings; danger has a face.
Greek hubris-and-fall — every great character is destroyed by the trait that made them great.
Buddhist bodhisattva vow — a soul that postpones nirvana to help others reach it; deferred completion as a gift.
Anansi the spider — West African trickster who collects all stories of the world and gives them back as gifts.
Genie in a lamp — three wishes; specificity is the tax; wording is the trap.
Ouroboros — snake eating its own tail; system that consumes itself to continue.

## How to use this list

When generating concepts in Step 3, the model picks Anchor and Delight moment values from this library. The bucket quota in `SKILL.md` requires the output set to draw from at least four of the ten buckets above (typically: 2 video games, 2 arts/cinema/music/literature/mythology, 2 history/sport/craft/architecture, 1 domestic/social, max 3 software).

The list is incomplete on purpose — add entries you discover in real use. The category headers stay; the entries grow.
