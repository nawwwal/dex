# Voice System

Use voice as product behavior, not a polish pass. Voice controls what the product chooses to name, explain, soften, prove, and refuse.

## Razorpay voice

Default voice: clear, composed, accountable, action-led, numerate, and founder-supportive.

Term -> meaning -> execution -> avoid:

- Clear -> the user can understand the state without translation -> use common words, exact product nouns, sentence case, and direct syntax -> jargon, filler, cleverness, and synonym churn.
- Composed -> the product stays calm when money or identity is involved -> use quiet punctuation, no panic, no jokes, no exclamation marks in serious flows -> "Oops", "Uh-oh", drama, or over-celebration.
- Accountable -> the copy names who can act or who caused the state -> say you, Razorpay, bank, customer, regulator, partner, or system when useful -> passive fog like "could not be processed".
- Action-led -> the sentence points to the next useful step -> start CTAs with verbs and pair states with recovery -> vague CTAs like Submit, Proceed, OK, or Confirm.
- Numerate -> numbers make financial systems trustworthy -> use exact amounts, limits, dates, durations, IDs, payment methods, and counts when relevant -> vague "soon", "some time", "many", or unsupported scale claims.
- Founder-supportive -> supports founders, startups, and entrepreneurs building under uncertainty -> name the concrete business action they can take now: accept payments, share a link, collect faster, track money, reduce manual work -> founder theater, "change the world" hype, or implying the work is effortless.

## Founder, startup, and entrepreneur lane

Razorpay often speaks to people trying to start, run, or scale a business. Make money movement feel possible, credible, and operationally manageable.

Use this lane for founder onboarding, first-payment flows, no-code payment links, early business setup, activation nudges, and product marketing surfaces.

Term -> meaning -> execution -> avoid:

- Startup-accessible -> helps a small team start without enterprise machinery -> say "no coding", "share over WhatsApp", "start collecting payments", or "track from the dashboard" when true -> enterprise-only language for early-stage users.
- Ambitious but grounded -> names the future the business wants and anchors it in mechanism -> connect growth to payment methods, conversion, recovery, settlement, reminders, or automation -> "unlock limitless growth".
- Operator-clear -> respects that the founder is also doing finance ops -> use objects, timelines, dashboard actions, and workflow verbs -> abstract productivity claims.
- Trust-building -> makes regulated money movement feel credible -> name proof, compliance boundary, partner-bank boundary, actor, or control -> casual reassurance without facts.
- Fast-to-value -> shows when value can start -> use "in minutes", "single click", or "no coding" only when the flow truly supports it -> speed claims blocked by KYC, bank, approval, or compliance steps.

## Voice by context

- Marketing: more aspirational, outcome-led, and proof-led.
- Onboarding: supportive, concrete, confidence-building.
- Product UI: concise, action-led, state-aware.
- High-risk finance: calm, exact, actor-aware.
- Legal/compliance: formal, precise, no cleverness.
- Developer/API: technical enough to be useful, but paired with exact fixes.

## Humor gate

Humor is a contextual tool, not a voice default.

Allow it only in low-risk moments where it does not carry essential meaning and will not become irritating through repetition.

Never use humor for payment failure, KYC rejection, settlement issues, refunds, chargebacks, disputes, account suspension, data loss, legal consent, destructive actions, or compliance review.

In Razorpay product copy, useful "joy" usually means reduced anxiety, visible progress, and confidence that money, identity, or data is handled correctly.
