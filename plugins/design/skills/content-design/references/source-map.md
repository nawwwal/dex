## Razorpay source grounding

Use the research file's Razorpay synthesis for default lanes:

- Platform authority: one platform, one dashboard, one provider, finance operating layer.
- Speed to value: no-code setup, instant collection, faster integration, single-click or minutes when true.
- Scale proof: payment methods, processed volume, adoption, success rates, cost/time saved.
- Operational control: dashboards, APIs, webhooks, reports, approvals, bulk actions, tracking.
- Growth outcome: conversion, fewer RTOs, more payment methods, easier collections, fewer manual steps.
- Trust and compliance: authorization, security, partner-bank boundaries, test/live mode, policy precision.

## External research fallback

Browse current sources when:

- The user asks for citations or source-backed rationale.
- A factual claim may be current-sensitive: pricing, regulation, RBI status, partner-bank language, product availability, payment methods, success rates, public proof numbers.
- Razorpay public marketing voice may have changed.
- The bundled references do not cover the industry, surface, or product area.
- A legal, compliance, or policy statement needs current wording.
- Comparative or superlative claims need proof.

Prefer official pages: Razorpay product pages and docs, official design systems, official policy pages, regulator pages, and primary company documentation.

Do not invent source authority. If current verification is needed and browsing is unavailable, say the claim needs verification before shipping.

## Source notes in output

Include source notes only when they help the user decide, when the user asks, or when the copy depends on a factual claim. Keep the note short: rule used, source family, and any current verification needed.
