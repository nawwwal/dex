# Getting Started with UX Reviewer (5F Edition)

**Quick Start Guide for the 5F-Integrated Design Critique Agent**

---

## ⚡ 60-Second Quick Start

1. **Invoke the skill:**
   - Say: "Review this design" or "Give me 5F analysis"
   - Upload your design screens

2. **Answer 7 questions:**
   - Screen context, target user, JTBD, ideal outcome, product context, design stage

3. **Pick your analysis:**
   - For B2B SaaS → Choose **"5F Framework Analysis"**
   - For comprehensive review → Choose **"Complete Analysis"**

4. **Select Soni's mood:**
   - Strategic, Creative Visionary, Critical Analyst, or Empathetic Coach

5. **Get actionable feedback:**
   - Scorecards with 1-5 ratings
   - Specific design recommendations
   - Optional Round 2 for edge cases

---

## 🆕 What's New: 5F Framework Integration

Your UX Reviewer now includes **The 5F Principles for B2B SaaS Design** - a research-backed framework built from real Indian B2B SaaS user insights.

### The 5F Principles

| F | Principle | What It Evaluates |
|---|-----------|-------------------|
| **F1** | **Make it Fast** | Proactive feedback, workflow tool integration, forgiving design, in-dashboard help |
| **F2** | **Make it Focused** | Information hierarchy (Glance >> Act >> Explore), cultural relevance, fight feature paralysis |
| **F3** | **Make it Fun** | Thought-speed performance, dopamine hits, dashboard personality, business impact |
| **F4** | **Make it Fluent** | Learning curve, persona customization, navigation memory, RBAC |
| **F5** | **Make it Fair** | Emotional intelligence, radical transparency, business language, AI trust |

## 🎯 Which Analysis Should You Choose?

### Option 1: Foundational Heuristic (Nielsen's 10)
**Best for:** Quick hygiene checks, universal UX audit
**Time:** 5-10 min
**Output:** 10-point scorecard with tactical fixes
**Use when:** You need fast, standards-based feedback

### Option 2: VP-Level Strategic (5 Pillars)
**Best for:** Business stakeholder presentations, strategic alignment
**Time:** 10-15 min
**Output:** 5-pillar scorecard connecting design to business metrics
**Use when:** You need to justify design decisions to execs

### Option 3: 5F Framework Analysis 🆕
**Best for:** B2B SaaS products (especially Indian market)
**Time:** 15-20 min
**Output:** 5F scorecard with market-specific insights
**Use when:**
- Your product is B2B SaaS (fintech, payments, ERP, CRM)
- Target users are Indian SME owners, ops managers, finance teams
- You need market-specific feedback (workflow tool integration patterns, Hinglish for Indian markets, social proof)
- Design involves dashboards or data-heavy interfaces

### Option 4-6: Combined Analysis
**Best for:** Comprehensive reviews before launch/major releases
**Time:** 20-30 min
**Output:** Multiple scorecards covering all frameworks
**Use when:** You want the complete picture (UX + strategy + market fit)

## 📋 The 7 Clarifying Questions

Soni will ask these before analysis. **Be specific!**

| # | Question | Good Answer Example | Bad Answer Example |
|---|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Screen Context | "This is the alert monitoring dashboard merchants land on after clicking 'Alerts' in the left nav. Before: home screen. After: alert detail view." | "It's a dashboard." |
| 2 | Target User | "Operations Manager at SME businesses, medium tech proficiency, motivated to catch payment failures ASAP to avoid revenue loss." | "Business users." |
| 3 | JTBD | "Identify critical payment alerts needing immediate attention and navigate to detailed view to resolve them." | "See alerts." |
| 4 | Ideal Outcome | "They spot high-priority alert within 5 seconds and click through to resolve it. Bonus: feel confident they're not missing anything." | "Find alerts." |
| 5 | Product Context | "Razorpay payment gateway. Screen's main function: real-time alert monitoring to prevent revenue loss." | "Fintech product." |
| 6 | Design Stage | "Experimentation Stage. Flow type: Exploration flow (scanning list of alerts)." | "Mid-stage design." |
| 7 | Market Context (optional) | "Indian B2B SaaS market, targeting tier 1 and tier 2 city merchants." | "Global." |

## 🇮🇳 5F Framework: When & Why

### Use 5F Framework When:

✅ **Your product is B2B SaaS:**
- Fintech, payments, banking
- ERP, CRM, HRMS
- Analytics, dashboards
- Workflow automation

✅ **Your market includes India:**
- SME business owners
- Operations managers
- Finance teams
- Tier 1 and tier 2 city users

✅ **Your design involves:**
- Dashboards with data-heavy interfaces
- Multi-role access (admin, manager, contributor)
- Trust-critical workflows (money, compliance, data)
- Mobile + desktop usage patterns

✅ **You need market-specific insights:**
- Workflow tool integration opportunities (identify which tools users use)
- Hinglish copy recommendations
- Social proof strategies
- Cultural iconography
- Low-bandwidth considerations

### Skip 5F Framework When:

❌ Product is B2C consumer app
❌ Market is exclusively Western/developed nations
❌ Design is purely branding/marketing (not product)
❌ Users are highly technical (developers, data scientists)

## 📊 Understanding Your Scorecards

### Scoring Scale (Applies to All Frameworks)

| Score | Meaning | What to Do |
|-------|---------|------------|
| **1** | Critical Flaw - Actively harms UX/business | **URGENT:** Redesign immediately |
| **2** | Major Improvement Needed - Significant gaps | **HIGH PRIORITY:** Fix before launch |
| **3** | Meets Expectations - Functional but not special | **MEDIUM:** Improve to differentiate |
| **4** | Strong Performance - Well-executed, user-centric | **LOW:** Polish and maintain |
| **5** | Exceptional/Industry-Leading - Competitive advantage | **CELEBRATE:** Document and replicate |

### Heuristic Scorecard (Nielsen's 10)
- 10 principles: Visibility, Match Real World, Control, Consistency, Error Prevention, Recognition, Efficiency, Minimalism, Error Recovery, Help
- Focus: Universal UX hygiene
- Quick scan for critical usability issues

### Strategic Scorecard (5 Pillars)
- 5 pillars: Strategic Alignment, Usability & Journey, Visual Design & Clarity, Content & Interaction, Innovation & Delight
- Focus: Business impact and competitive positioning
- Connects design choices to metrics (activation, retention, efficiency)

### 5F Scorecard (B2B SaaS)
- 5 principles: Fast, Focused, Fun, Fluent, Fair
- Focus: Market-specific design excellence
- Evidence-based from Indian B2B SaaS user research
- Addresses cultural context, trust-building, and business outcomes

## 💡 Example Use Cases

### Use Case 1: SME Merchant Dashboard (Indian Fintech)
**Scenario:** Payment gateway dashboard for Indian merchants to monitor transactions

**Recommended Analysis:** **5F Framework** or **Heuristic + 5F**

**Why:**
- B2B SaaS product (fintech)
- Indian SME target users
- Dashboard with data-heavy interface
- Trust is critical (money involved)

**What you'll get:**
- F1 (Fast): Proactive error warnings, workflow tool integration opportunities (Slack/WhatsApp/Teams/email)
- F2 (Focused): Information hierarchy critique (Glance >> Act >> Explore), cultural icon suggestions
- F3 (Fun): Dashboard personality assessment (operational anchor vs performance enabler), business impact visualization ideas
- F4 (Fluent): Persona customization gaps (owner view vs accountant view), navigation memory issues
- F5 (Fair): Transparency gaps in transaction tracking, business language vs jargon analysis

---

### Use Case 2: Global SaaS CRM (Western Market)
**Scenario:** CRM tool for sales teams in US/Europe

**Recommended Analysis:** **Heuristic + Strategic** (skip 5F)

**Why:**
- B2B SaaS but Western market
- Cultural relevance of 5F less applicable
- Standard UX + business strategy more relevant

**What you'll get:**
- Nielsen heuristics for UX hygiene
- Strategic pillars for competitive differentiation
- Skip market-specific recommendations (tool integrations, Hinglish, social proof)

---

### Use Case 3: Experimental AI Feature (Exploration Stage)
**Scenario:** AI-powered invoice parser for accountants (early prototype)

**Recommended Analysis:** **Complete Analysis** with **Empathetic Coach** mood

**Why:**
- Exploration stage = need bold, innovative ideas
- AI feature = needs trust framework (5F F5)
- B2B SaaS = benefits from all frameworks

**What you'll get:**
- Wild AI trust framework ideas (confidence scores, source tracing)
- Research-backed recommendations (XAI, Copilot patterns)
- Strategic innovation critique
- Cultural adaptation opportunities

## 🔄 Getting the Most from Round 2

After your first scorecard, Soni will ask: **"Would you like to see another version?"**

**Say YES when:**
- Design is complex or multi-step flow
- First round felt like it missed something
- You want edge case analysis
- Using 5F and want market-specific deep dive

**Round 2 gives you:**
- **No repeated points** from Round 1
- Edge cases (alert overload, multi-store scenarios, mobile considerations)
- Market-specific scenarios (tier-2 cities, low bandwidth, mobile-first users with preferred tools)
- Missed opportunities from first analysis

## 🎯 Success Tips

### Before the Session
1. **Know your user:** Have persona details ready
2. **Know your goal:** What metric does this design need to move?
3. **Know your stage:** Be honest about Exploration vs Final UI
4. **Prepare screens:** Complete flows, not isolated screens

### During the Session
1. **Be specific:** Detailed answers > vague one-liners
2. **Admit unknowns:** "I don't know yet" is better than guessing
3. **Take notes:** Capture specific recommendations
4. **Ask follow-ups:** "How would you fix the error prevention issue?"

### After the Session
1. **Prioritize by score:** Fix 1s and 2s first
2. **Tie to metrics:** Connect recommendations to business KPIs
3. **Share with team:** Use scorecards in design reviews
4. **Iterate fast:** Re-submit after major fixes for validation

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

### "Soni is skipping the 7 questions"
**Solution:** Your initial prompt was too detailed. Soni auto-filled answers. Confirm they're correct or ask to redo.

### "I want 5F but got Nielsen heuristics"
**Solution:** Double-check your analysis choice in Phase 3. Explicitly say "5F Framework Analysis."

### "Scores seem too harsh"
**Solution:** Check design stage. Final UI gets harsher critique than Exploration. Adjust mood to "Creative Visionary" for encouragement.

### "I don't understand a 5F principle"
**Solution:** Read `5F-FRAMEWORK-REFERENCE.md` for full details, user verbatims, and design examples.

### "Recommendations are too generic"
**Solution:** Provide more specific context in clarifying questions. Include business metrics, user pain points, competitor landscape.
