## Culture Competencies

### We are customer first
- Advocate for user experience (UX): Promote usability and accessibility considerations during design discussions.
- Develop features with prototypes: Build low-fidelity prototypes to get early user feedback and iterate based on their input.
- Write user stories: Contribute to user stories that clearly define user needs and desired outcomes.
- Connect with Customers: Interact with at least 5 customers in a review cycle either via FreshDesk or shadowing support calls
- Conduct user interviews: Participate in user interviews to gain firsthand understanding of user needs and challenges.
- Contribute to customer support: Offer occasional assistance to the customer support team to gain insights into real-world issues.
- Champion A/B testing: Encourage A/B testing of different design options to determine what works best for users.

### We take ownership
- Offer alternative solutions: When encountering challenges, present well-researched options alongside potential drawbacks, advocating for the most efficient approach.
- Take initiative on small bugs: Actively fix minor bugs and document your process, learning from the experience and taking full ownership of the resolution.
- Seek stretch assignments: Volunteer for tasks slightly beyond your current skillset, demonstrating your willingness to learn and grow in your ownership.

### We challenge the status quo
- Identify inefficiencies: Proactively analyze workflows and suggest solutions to streamline processes or improve code structure.
- Research alternative solutions: When encountering problems, research alternative approaches beyond the usual fixes. Present the findings and discuss their feasibility.
- Contribute to innovation discussions: Participate actively in brainstorming sessions and offer unconventional ideas, even if they seem risky at first.

### We internalize agility and integrity
- Propose alternative approaches: When encountering challenges, present at least two potential solutions, highlighting their pros and cons, and recommend the most efficient path forward.
- Own your learning: Actively seek opportunities to learn new technologies or tools relevant to current projects and suggest their application for improvement.
- Champion small changes: Advocate for streamlining repetitive tasks or improving communication channels within your team, proposing specific actionable steps.

### We are transparent
General principles (all levels):
- Share relevant information proactively: Share information that impacts your team or projects through established channels, avoiding information overload.
- Be open to questions and feedback: Encourage colleagues to ask questions and provide feedback, creating a safe space for open communication.
- Respect privacy and confidentiality: Recognize that not all information needs to be public and respect boundaries around sensitive topics.

Level-specific:
- Lead by example: Actively share relevant information about your work and encourage others to do the same.
- Organize information sharing sessions: Facilitate discussions or presentations to share broader project updates or learnings with the team.
- Advocate for clear communication channels: Champion well-defined and accessible communication channels for different types of information.

### Razorpay grows with Razors
- Contribute to discussions: Actively participate in design meetings and suggest alternative solutions, even if they feel unconventional.
- Propose improvements: Identify areas in your codebase or workflow that could be optimized and suggest concrete improvements.
- Seek new challenges: Volunteer for tasks outside your comfort zone to expand your skillset and knowledge.

## Technical Competencies

### Role Summary

A strong design executor + an intermediate product solver. Takes part ownership of product solutioning along with complete accountability for great design outcomes on their projects.

**Typical Charter:** Product Experience of POD

### Problem Complexity

- Validated problem statement
- The product solution has a direction but is not detailed
- Often a feature within a product or a small product
- Few overlaps with other products to handle
- May require deep product domain context
- Requires design solution beyond just functionality, at a behavioural and visceral level

### Core Competencies

#### Product Thinking

**Problem identification & prioritization**

Supports progress towards achieving the OKRs by:
- Assessing/questioning the prioritized problems and their potential impact
- Bringing in insights from previous researches, data and recordings

**Product Solutioning**

Ensures the right solution direction has been taken by:
- Asking the right questions from a user lens
- Validating and guiding product decisions through user insights

#### UX Design

**UX creativity**

Consistently delivers user-friendly solutions that meet benchmarks by delivering designs derived out of:
- Data and recording analysis of current user behaviour
- User insights gathered with guidance
- Validation of designs through hallway or user testing

**Functional Solutions**

Solves all possible use and edge cases within the current scope of the problem through:
- Detailed understanding and jotting down of the ideal user journey
- Pre-mortem exercise to ensure 95-100% coverage of cases

#### UI Design

**Aesthetic quality**

Demonstrates instances of creating UI that elevate the standard within their team by:
- Catering to the tech literacy of the user persona type (basic v/s power) in UI patterns
- Exploring beyond basic UI elements, for example, transitions, micro-interactions

**Usage of & contribution to design system**

Demonstrates instances of contributing to the Design system through:
- (as needed) Creation of new components
- (as needed) Enhancements to existing components

### Behavioural Competencies

#### Communication & Collaboration

Drives buy-in for their ideas with stakeholders, and is able to run design discussions or alignment with wider forums by:
- Conveying design decisions through a well-crafted presentation
- Driving product/design decisions through evangelisation of user insights within the POD

#### Ownership & Delivery

**Ownership**

Ensures that the experience for the user in their charter is top-notch, through:
- Maintenance of and active work on a design backlog, beyond their day-to-day project

**Planning and Delivery**

Achieves on-time and quality implementation of projects by:
- Collaboratively planning with DEP, and coordinating with developers

#### Leadership

**Mentorship**

Contributes to the growth of individual designers and team culture by:
- Sharing constructive feedback to help designers in the team
- Buddying new designers in the team

**Team Building**

Contributes to team-wide activities frequently with guidance on items like:
- External talks
- Contribution to wider activities like Town hall, Offsite, All hands, etc.

## Career Framework

### Impact
A PD II is a strong design executor and intermediate product solver. They take part ownership of product solutioning alongside complete accountability for great design outcomes on their projects. Impact is focused on delivering user-friendly solutions for features or small products with validated problem statements, where design goes beyond functionality to address behavioral and visceral quality.

They independently drive design from research through handoff — scoping the problem, exploring multiple directions, covering 95–100% of use and edge cases, and delivering well-crafted presentations that drive stakeholder alignment. Their work demonstrates measurable craft quality (Blade Score, iteration depth, prototype fidelity) and is validated through hallway testing or user testing before shipping.

### Leadership
A PD II contributes to the growth of individual designers and team culture. They share constructive feedback to help other designers improve, and buddy new designers joining the team. They contribute to team-wide activities with guidance — external talks, town halls, offsites, all-hands.

They drive buy-in for their design ideas with stakeholders through well-crafted presentations and are capable of running design discussions or alignment sessions with wider forums. They mentor by example — demonstrating the level of design craft, ownership, and user-centricity expected at PD II.

Taking initiative rather than waiting.
- **Collaboration & Stakeholder Alignment** -- Appears across culture (transparent communication, information sharing), technical (driving buy-in, design discussions, coordinating with developers), and career framework (collaborate with stakeholders, build confidence). Working effectively across functions.
- **Continuous Improvement & Learning** -- Appears across culture (seek stretch assignments, own your learning, challenge status quo), technical (contributing to design system, exploring beyond basics), and career framework (learn from peers, set high benchmarks, improve practices). Growth mindset is expected.
- **Mentorship & Team Building** -- Appears across culture (Razorpay grows with Razors), technical (mentorship, buddying, team building), and career framework (onboard and mentor, team's success over individual). Contributing to others' growth alongside your own.
