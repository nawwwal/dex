# Brand Direction Brief (Pre-Diverge)

**Product:** Ledgerly — invoicing and payouts for Indian freelancers and micro-agencies  
**Stage:** Rebrand exploration before `/diverge` concept generation  
**Requested output:** Grounded brand direction inputs — not final identity, but constraints and hypotheses to explore

## Why rebrand now

- Current visual system reads "generic SaaS dashboard" (blue gradients, Inter, stock illustrations)
- Competing with Refrens and Zoho on features; need emotional differentiation for trust-sensitive fintech
- Onboarding research shows users don't perceive us as "serious enough" for bank linking
- Website and in-product visuals have diverged; no shared token system

## Audience

**Primary:** Freelancers and solo consultants (22–38) in metros, ₹3–15L annual revenue  
**Secondary:** Small agencies (2–8 people) managing client invoicing  
**Emotional job:** "Feel professional without feeling corporate"  
**Anxiety:** Getting paid on time, GST compliance, sharing bank details with a new tool

## Brand attributes (draft)

| We want to feel | We do not want to feel |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Calm, competent | Flashy fintech bro |
| Local, grounded | Silicon Valley clone |
| Transparent | Compliance-scary |
| Warm but precise | Cute or playful |

## Color direction (hypotheses, not decisions)

**Hypothesis A — Earth + ink**  
Warm stone backgrounds (`#F5F0E8`), deep ink text (`#1A1A2E`), accent saffron (`#E8A317`) for CTAs. Signals Indian warmth without cliché tricolor.

**Hypothesis B — Monochrome trust**  
Near-black and off-white with single green accent (`#0F7B5C`) for money/success states. Closer to banking apps; risks feeling cold.

**Hypothesis C — Soft professional**  
Slate blue-gray palette with coral accent for urgency. Current direction evolved; team is bored of it.

Open question: Does saffron read "Indian fintech" or "discount banner" at button scale?

## Typography direction

- **Current:** Inter everywhere; legible but anonymous
- **Exploring:**  
  - Display: Instrument Serif or Fraunces for headlines (warmth, editorial)  
  - UI: DM Sans or Plus Jakarta Sans (modern, not overused in fintech)  
  - Numbers/tabular: IBM Plex Mono for invoice amounts and account numbers
- **Constraint:** Must support Devanagari numerals in invoices eventually; Latin-first for Q2

## Tone of voice

- Short sentences. No jargon unless legally required.
- Prefer "your money" over "funds" or "disbursements"
- Errors explain what happened and what to do next
- Avoid: "Oops!", "Awesome!", "Supercharge your workflow"

**Sample headline tension:**
- Too soft: "Getting paid should feel good"
- Too hard: "Enterprise-grade payment infrastructure"
- Target band: "Send invoices. Get paid. Stay compliant."

## Competitive visual landscape

| Brand | Visual read | Our takeaway |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Razorpay | Bold, developer-trust | Too enterprise for freelancer wedge |
| Refrens | Friendly, cluttered | Warm but low-trust for bank step |
| Zoho | Functional, dated | Safe, forgettable |
| Stripe | Minimal, global | Aspirational but not local |

## Deliverables before diverge

1. Agree on 2–3 color hypotheses to explore (not one winner)
2. Lock typography pairing for exploration frames
3. Define "trust moment" visual pattern for bank verification screen
4. List anti-patterns to reject in diverge (gradients, illustration style, mascot)
